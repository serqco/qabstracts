An onboarding model for integrating newcomers into agile project teams.

Context:
{{h-background}}
A stable team is deemed optimal for agile software development project success; however, all teams
change membership over time.
{{background}}
Newcomers joining an agile project team must rapidly assimilate into the
organisational and project environment.
{{background}}
They must do this while learning how to contribute effectively and
without seriously interrupting project progress.
{{background}}
Objective:
{{h-objective}}
This paper addresses how newcomers integrate into an established agile project team and how agile
practices assist with onboarding.
{{objective}}
Method:
{{h-method}}
A single, qualitative case study approach was used, investigating a co-located agile project team in a
large IT department who regularly onboard inexperienced newcomers.
{{method:i1}}
Analysis was abductive, consisting of
inductive coding and theming using categories from an existing onboarding theory.
{{method}}
Results:
{{h-result}}
We describe the team's onboarding practices and adjustments and present an agile onboarding model
that encompasses onboarding activities, individual adjustments, and workplace adjustments.
{{result}}
Conclusions:
{{h-conclusion}}
A mixture of general and specific agile onboarding practices contribute to successful onboarding in
an agile team.
{{conclusion}}
We provide practical guidelines to improve onboarding practice in agile teams.
{{a-result}}
Our major new
contribution is an extended model of onboarding for agile teams.
{{a-result}}
